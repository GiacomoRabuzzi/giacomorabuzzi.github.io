R State Space Models Kalman Filter Importance Sampling

State Space Methods for Electoral Polling

Master's thesis at Università Milano-Bicocca. Developed a family of state-space models to aggregate Italian political polls (2018–2024), extending the Pelagatti–Monti framework in three directions: institute-specific house effects, heteroskedastic measurement variances linked to sample size, and a structural covariance matrix capturing methodological affinities across polling organisations.

Estimation relies on Kalman filtering, Durbin–Koopman approximations for binomial observations, and importance sampling for corrected credible intervals. Results show variance is the main driver of discrepancies across institutes, while house effects are modest (±1 pp) and party-specific. A handful of institutes — notably Ixè, Lab2101, and Tecnè — display persistent structural tilts. Smoothed vote-share trends align with well-known Italian electoral dynamics.

Drought Prediction Using Weather Data

Applied regression, classification, and machine learning models — including Random Forest — to weather and drought datasets to evaluate whether automated methods can support or replace expert interpretation in drought monitoring and analysis.

The study compared traditional statistical approaches with machine learning techniques, assessing their predictive capabilities and reliability. Drought effects are often underestimated yet critically impact agriculture, water supply, energy production, and food security. The project explored how far data-driven models can reduce the human effort required for ongoing drought analysis.

R Neural Networks K-Means IoT Data

Presence Monitoring to Reduce Energy Waste

Investigated indoor occupancy monitoring using non-invasive environmental sensors (CO₂, temperature, and lighting) supported by machine learning. The goal: optimize energy efficiency without dedicated occupancy hardware.

After thorough exploratory analysis, clustering algorithms were used to identify occupancy patterns, and classification algorithms estimated the number of occupants. K-means demonstrated excellent clustering capabilities while Neural Networks achieved superior classification performance. Evaluation considered both effectiveness and computational efficiency.

2023
R EDDA MDA Classification

NBA Position Classification & Basketball's Positionless Evolution

Two-objective study using NBA shooting data from 1997–98 to the present. First, classified basketball players by position using generative probabilistic models (EDDA and MDA). Second, assessed whether basketball is increasingly moving away from traditional positional roles.

The dataset was divided into three historical "eras" and classification models trained on one era were tested on the others — revealing how well positional labels transfer across time. Results speak to the ongoing "positionless basketball" debate, offering a data-driven perspective on how the sport has evolved structurally over three decades.
