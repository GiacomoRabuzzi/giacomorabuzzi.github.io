R State Space Models Kalman Filter Importance Sampling

State Space Methods for Electoral Polling

When you walk out of the polling station and check your phone, you already find ten different polls telling ten different things. Who's right? This thesis starts exactly from that question. Working with over 12,000 political polls published in Italy between 2018 and 2024 — a period that saw four governments, a pandemic, and a dramatic reshuffling of the political landscape — I built statistical models capable of separating signal from noise: distinguishing real public opinion from the imperfections of those who measure it. The most surprising finding? Differences between polling institutes come far more from random error than from systematic distortion. In other words, pollsters aren't "cheating" — measuring public opinion is just genuinely hard. That said, a few institutes stand out: Tecnè, for instance, consistently overestimates Forza Italia — a pattern that's hard not to connect to the fact that its polls are frequently broadcast on Mediaset channels. The estimated trends tell a precise political story too: the rise and fall of Lega, the unstoppable climb of Fratelli d'Italia to electoral leadership, the resilience of PD, the collapse and stabilisation of M5S. All of this through a transparent, replicable framework — successfully applied to the Austrian case as well.

R Random Forest Regression Classification

Drought Prediction Using Weather Data

Can a machine replace a meteorologist? We put it to the test. Using weather data from thousands of U.S. counties, we trained a range of statistical and machine learning models — including Random Forest — to predict drought conditions without any human interpretation. Spoiler: it's harder than it sounds. Droughts are sneaky — they affect agriculture, water supply, energy and food security, yet the data is messy, imbalanced, and full of edge cases. Our models got surprisingly good at detecting whether drought was present or absent, but struggled to nail down severity. The conclusion? Automation can genuinely support expert work — but isn't ready to replace it just yet.

R Neural Networks K-Means IoT Data

Presence Monitoring to Reduce Energy Waste

Lights left on in an empty room. AC running with nobody there. Small inefficiencies, big waste. This project tackled a deceptively simple question: can you tell how many people are in a room using just CO₂ levels, temperature, and light — no cameras, no badges, no dedicated hardware? Turns out, yes — remarkably well. After exploring patterns in sensor data, we tested clustering and classification algorithms to estimate occupancy in real time. Neural Networks came out on top for accuracy, while K-means proved the cleanest and most efficient clustering approach. A practical, privacy-friendly blueprint for smarter buildings.

NBA Position Classification & Basketball's Positionless Evolution

Is "positionless basketball" actually happening, or is it just commentary hype? We went to the data to find out. Using NBA shooting statistics from 1997 to today, we built classification models to predict a player's position purely from how and where they shoot — no names, no scouting reports. The models worked well for guards and centers, but forwards? Almost impossible to pin down — they really do play like everyone else. Then we split the data into three historical eras and tested models across them: a classifier trained on the late 90s barely recognizes today's players. The game has genuinely changed — and the numbers prove it.
